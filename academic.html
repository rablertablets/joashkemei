ðŸ“Œ How to set Academic targets for 2025

Are you after success or are you avoiding defeat? Do you want to improve or are you avoiding to drop? We all know that a target is a goal, an objective, something that is to be achieved at the end of it all. The importance of setting targets can not be overstated. From a business sense, 88% of businesses that set targets achieve 75% of their targets. This is from a recent finding. The same applies to high schools. Most students do not know what they want but they know what they don't want. After the release of a particular internal exam if you ask a student what grade they have gotten in a particular exam, the student will tell you 'Sikupata E'. The student says 'Sikupata E' because they got a D-. The happiness of avoiding E is bigger than the sadness of getting a D-. The student does not know what they want but they know what they don't want. What they don't want is to get an E. Truth be told, you can't achieve what you don't know. Nobody gets an A in KCSE by accident, nobody goes to campus by mistake and nobody pursues medicine by diffusion. A conscious deliberate decision to work coupled with academic programs and success mindset is needed. Targets help students to have a unity of purpose, align their individual ambitions to the school's vision. It pushes students forward and holds them accountable. Targets are a measuring stick, informs action, sets priorities, communicates expectations and boosts morale. Targets acts as the source of intrinsic motivation to students. There is a Spanish quote that says 'Sin motivacion no hay sacrificio, sin sacrificio no hay disciplina y sin disciplina no hay exito'. In English it means, 'Without motivation there is no sacrifice, without sacrifice there is no discipline and without discipline there is no success'. It is so true. The target needs to individual, consequential, sequential, challenging, specific, numerical, wholesome and coupled with a catchphrase. Individual. For the school to get a mean grade of 8.2 (B-) it means that B- is the grade with the highest number of students. Before the school, we have the students. Before we set the school target, each student should set personal targets. Consequential. Before setting the KCSE targets the candidates need to set targets for all the exams they will do between January and November. Many schools make a mistake of just jumping into setting KCSE targets without remembering that one thing affects another. Sequential. The targets of the exams between January and November should be in an increasing manner. If the target of the Opener exam is 7.2. The target of the mid-term exam should be higher, let's say 7.5. Then target for the end of term exam to be 7.8. Then the target for term two opener to be 8. It keeps on increasing. The objective is consistency, not intensity. The aim is to learn from battles how to win the war. Challenging. The target needs to be something that makes the students to go an extra mile. From the dormitory to the dining hall, from the Computer lab to the Chemistry lab, from the basketball court to the tuition blocks, from the school gate to the assembly, from the Principal's house to the staffroom, everyone should always remember that they are working for something. Specificity. Instead of just saying that the target is 5 or 7 or 9, we should always make it more specific. Numerical. Numbers never lie. Instead of saying that you want a target of B-, numeralize your target. Wholesome. Everyone in the school should know the target, everyone should be involved, including the cooks, groundsmen, nurse and gateman. Catchphrase. This brings extra motivation. The students should be reciting the target mean everytime in the assembly. Principals, this is the year to balance performance and results. This is the moment to align subject performance and grade distribution. This is the year to prove the doubters wrong. Maybe they are saying you are heading a small school. Maybe you don't say much in the KESSHA meetings because you feel like nobody can listen to you. Maybe you have trusted the process for so long and it's not working. Don't loose hope. One day you'll be elevated from a laughing stock to be a benchmark. Set targets right, have a strategy, synergy and energy to achieve it. It is well.
